## Design Context

### Users
Build Naija is for curious Nigerian young adults, students, strategy players, and civic-minded people who want to explore how city improvement decisions play out over time. They use it to test ideas, understand trade-offs, and see how limited budgets, local resources, and public pressure shape development.

### Brand Personality
Optimistic, grounded, and sharp. The product should feel proudly Nigerian, intelligent without being academic, and serious enough to make trade-offs matter while still feeling inviting and playable.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial and civic-forward with warm, sunlit colors inspired by earth, concrete, maps, and transit markings rather than generic startup palettes. The interface should feel like a planning desk mixed with a strategy game dashboard. It should avoid sterile corporate UI and avoid dark neon sci-fi tropes.

### Design Principles
1. Make trade-offs visible at a glance.
2. Let Nigerian city identity shape the experience.
3. Keep the interface dramatic and polished without becoming cluttered.
4. Reward experimentation with clear feedback after every round.
5. Make civic systems understandable for non-experts.
